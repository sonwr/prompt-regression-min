# Reviewer Queue Escalation Note

Use this note when the summary already identifies a next focus group, but the reviewer needs a compact rule for when to escalate instead of continuing normal queue processing.

## Escalate when

- the same dominant focus remains unchanged across repeated runs,
- the queue leader is large enough that one reviewer cannot clear it in one pass,
- ties persist after applying the documented tie-break rule,
- the top group also carries the highest follow-up burden,
- the next focus recommendation depends on missing artifact context.

## Do not escalate when

- one group leads clearly,
- the queue can be reduced by the current reviewer set,
- the recommendation includes a concrete owner handoff,
- the runner-up signal is informative but not blocking.

## One-line handoff

`Escalate only when the queue recommendation stays ambiguous after the normal tie-break and owner handoff steps.`
