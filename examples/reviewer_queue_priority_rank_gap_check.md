# Reviewer queue priority-rank gap check

Use this quick check when `priority_rank` exists but the lead still feels too narrow to post without context.

## What to confirm

1. The selected queue has the best exposed `priority_rank`.
2. The lead is supported by queue share or source-case rate, not just ordering.
3. The runner-up remains visible when the gap is small.

## Copy-ready audit

- **Leader** — the queue with the best visible `priority_rank`
- **Gap** — how far ahead the leader is from the runner-up
- **Support** — queue share, source-case rate, or both
- **Decision** — post a solo handoff, or keep the runner-up in the note

## Suggested wording

- Clear lead: `P1 stays first; its rank lead is supported by the largest queue share.`
- Narrow lead: `P1 stays first, but the rank gap is narrow, so keep P2 visible in the handoff.`
- Tie-like lead: `The exposed rank still points to P1, but the gap is thin enough to treat the handoff as P1-first with P2 kept in view.`
